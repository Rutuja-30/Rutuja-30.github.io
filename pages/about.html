About me

I developed an interest in biomedical engineering when I saw my grandmother regain mobility after knee replacement surgery. Watching how medical technology could restore someone’s independence made me realize the real impact engineering can have on people’s lives. That experience inspired me to pursue work that combines engineering with healthcare.

Through hands-on projects and research, I have gained experience in medical device prototyping, experimental testing, verification and validation, and biomedical data analysis. Also, got exposure to design controls and quality-driven development. These experiences have helped me develop a strong foundation in building and evaluating healthcare technologies that address real clinical needs.

Beyond technical work, I have explored the broader healthcare ecosystem through clinical research and interviews with caregivers, nurses, and healthcare technology leaders to better understand challenges in elderly care. I am always looking for opportunities that allow me to learn, grow, and contribute to meaningful medical technology development.
